The Golden Rule Across Religions

Christianity: Do to others what you would have them do to you.

Judaism: Love your neighbor as yourself.

Islam: None of you truly believes until he loves for his brother what he loves for himself.

Hinduism: This is the sum of duty; do nothing to others which would cause you pain if done to you.

Buddhism: Hurt not others in ways that you yourself would find hurtful.

Confucianism: Do not impose on others what you do not wish for yourself.

Zoroastrianism: That nature alone is good which refrains from doing unto another whatsoever is not good for itself.

Taoism: Treat all with goodness and faithfulness.

Sikhism: Speak sweetly to all and never in anger.

Bahá'í Faith: Choose for your neighbor that which you choose for yourself.
